Why Organizations Call Us

Your insurance carrier is requiring one.

Carriers are adding Emergency Action Plan requirements to commercial policies. No plan means higher premiums, policy conditions — or denied coverage. Your wallet is already on the line.

OSHA requires it — and the fines are real.

OSHA 29 CFR 1910.38 requires a written EAP for most workplaces. Violations run up to $15,625 per citation — and up to $156,259 for willful non-compliance. Ignorance is not a defense.

When something happens, no plan means liability.

If an employee or student is injured during an emergency and you had no documented plan, no training, and no drills — your exposure in court is significant. A plan isn't just compliance. It's protection.

You have a plan but it's never been tested.

A plan sitting in a binder is not a plan. If your staff has never trained on it and your facility has never run a drill, you don't have an emergency plan — you have a document. We fix that.

Your school board or stakeholders are asking.

Schools face increasing pressure to demonstrate active emergency preparedness — especially for fire, lockdown, and severe weather events. A documented, tested EAP satisfies your board, your community, and your state.

You don't know where to start.

Most organizations don't have a certified emergency planner on staff. That's exactly what we are. We handle everything — the writing, the training, the exercises, and the updates — so you don't have to figure it out alone.
